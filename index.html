Spreading the News

Before the rise of Internet and technologies like the telephone, information would spread very differently than when compared to today. Currently we have almost instantaneous access to information about events going on across the world. This information comes in such a large stream as well that people are able to discover incredible insights from it, such as the Northeastern University researchers using Twitter to predict the spread of flu season.

In 1835 however, news would spread very differently. Going out across all available travel ways through newspapers and couriers. The starkest difference between then and now is the time it would take to receive the information. One example of this would be the CSS Shenandoah a ship that operated as apart of the Confederate States Navy, during the American Civil War. The Shenandoah actually fought until six months after the American Civil War ended, because they were executing on news that was outdated. The Shenandoah actually learned of the Confederacy's surrender off of the coast of California after which disposed of their cannons and repainted their ship. Sailing to Liverpool, England in hope of being treated fairly.
